# 小作文（柱状图）练习

连接词例句
followed by、其次依次是The orange consumption for each person in Denmark was 31 kilograms approximately,followed by 27 kilograms in Spain,20 kilograms in Italy,and around 17 kilograms in Austria.、丹麦人均柑橘消费量约为 31 公斤，其次是西班牙 27 公斤，意大利 20 公斤，奥地利约 17 公斤。
by far、显然… 得多，大大地I agree with you that the open system is by far the best.、我同意你的观点，开放的系统显然是最佳的。
by a long shot、根本，远远Sales of girls 'toys are outpacing boys' sales by a long shot so far.、目前，女孩玩具销售额已经远远超过超过了男孩。
per capita、人均的They have the world's largest per capita income.、他们的人均收入居世界之首。
whereas、鉴于（句首），然而，尽管（对比两个事实）The research of image and video is very important whereas their characteristic.、鉴于图象和视频数据的这些特点，对图象和视频的研究就显得尤为重要。、Some of the studies show positive results, whereas others do not.、有一些研究结果令人满意，然而其他的则不然。

# 2022/04/23

The chart below shows the consumption of apples and oranges per person in kilograms in five countries in 2013.

Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

Write at least 150 words.

雅思小作文总结段放在第二段或者最后一段都可以

The bar chart illustrates how many apples and oranges consumed respectively by people in five nations in Europe by the year 2013.

Overall,the consumption of oranges per person was higher than that of apple in Austria,Turkey and Spain.By contrast,the Italian preferred apples rather than oranges,while people in Denmark have the same preference for the two fruits.

Regarding apples,the consumption per capita in Denmark was the largest by a long shot,at slightly over 30 kilograms whereas the quantity in Spain was the least,at about 7 kilograms.In Italy,people ate 25 kilograms per person, and this figure was nearly twice higher than that in Austria and Turkey ,at roughly 13 kilograms and 12 kilograms respectively.

When it comes to oranges,the Turkish ate considerably more than those in any other country,at 37 kilograms.The orange consumption for each person in Denmark was 31 kilograms approximately,followed by 27 kilograms in Spain,20 kilograms in Italy,and around 17 kilograms in Austria.
